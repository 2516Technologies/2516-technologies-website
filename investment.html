First: what this build is actually worth at market rate

This platform requires one person to simultaneously act as cloud architect, senior full-stack developer, DevOps engineer, data engineer, and product lead — for 4+ months of serious, sustained work. In the market, that scope is typically handled by a 3–5 person team or a senior fractional CTO plus contractors. The pricing below reflects a meaningful discount from what you would pay anyone else for equivalent capability and accountability.

This is important context — not a negotiating tactic. Understanding where these numbers come from helps you evaluate what you're getting, make an informed comparison to other proposals you receive, and have a clear conversation about scope if anything changes.

★ What Options 1 & 2 cannot offer — ever

Options 1 and 2 are one-time builds. Once 2516 hands over the code, your platform is frozen in time — it will not improve unless you pay for more development. Security vulnerabilities will accumulate. The Claude model powering your chatbot will become outdated. AWS will release better services that your platform won't use. You won't know about any of it until something breaks or a client asks why a competitor's tool is faster.

Option 3 is different because 2516 has a stake in your platform's success. The monthly reports, proactive upgrade recommendations, annual roadmap reviews, and dedicated Slack access aren't perks — they're the reason the platform stays excellent over time. A platform that gets better every quarter retains users. A platform that stagnates loses them. That difference, compounded over three years at 1,000+ users, is worth far more than the gap in upfront cost.
