Our initial assessment
⚠️
The prototype cannot support 1,000 users
The Claude-based prototype you and Ari built is almost certainly storing data in flat JSON with no real backend. That works for two people demoing a concept. It fails at any meaningful concurrency — and it cannot support analytics, billing, multi-tenancy, or AI model training at any scale. This is a rebuild, not an upgrade. That's not a criticism — it's what prototypes are for.
📱
Mobile isn't optional for this market
Small nonprofit staff are frequently part-time, volunteer-run, and phone-first. A platform that doesn't work excellently on a 375px screen will lose the majority of its target users before they ever log in a second time. Mobile-first design is a core architecture decision, not a CSS adjustment made at the end.
🔢
Four product surfaces need a clear build sequence
The virtual consultant, avatar, chatbot, and CRM are four distinct surfaces with different technical dependencies. Building all four simultaneously is the fastest way to deliver something that looks bad and doesn't work — exactly what you said gives you a stomach ache. The right approach is a phased build: stable foundation first, AI features second, after the infrastructure is proven.
✅
The concept is proven — the prototype did its job
The Claude API integration works. The UX vision is clear. You have something to demo to stakeholders and early clients right now. None of that work is wasted — the prompt architecture, the CRM interface intent, and the conversation patterns all carry forward into the production build.
💡
The platform needs to be built to grow — not just to launch
In 12 months you'll want behavior analytics. In 18 months you'll want to train a custom AI model on your users' data. In 24 months you'll want grant scoring and donor recommendations. Every one of those capabilities requires clean, structured data collected from day one. The architecture we're proposing makes all of it possible without a rebuild.
