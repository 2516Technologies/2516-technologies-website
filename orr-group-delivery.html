Delivery Options

Three ways to engage 2516. The difference matters more than the price.

The right option isn't the cheapest one it's the one that matches how much operational responsibility Orr Group wants to own. This page is intentionally direct about what each option means in practice.

Option 01
Build, Deliver & Train

2516 builds the full platform, delivers the codebase, and trains Orr Group's team. After the 90-day post-launch window, the engagement is complete. Orr Group owns the code, the IP, and all operational responsibility.

Orr Group's inherited responsibilities
AWS cost monitoring + budget management
The Orr Group's AWS account. The Orr Group's bill. Variable costs scale with usage someone needs to watch this and act on anomalies.
User onboarding + account management
Every new nonprofit subscriber needs onboarding. That's a real operational burden at 1,000+ organizations.
Platform updates + dependency patching
Dependencies go stale. Security patches need applying. Without this, the platform becomes a liability over time.
Security monitoring + incident response
No proactive monitoring means incidents become crises. Someone needs to own this or engage 2516 at $175/hr when something breaks.
All vendor relationships
AWS, Stripe, and any third-party integrations are Orr Group's relationship to manage. 2516 is no longer in the picture.

Staffing requirement: This option requires a part-time technical operator ($65K/yr) to manage AWS, security, patching, and vendor relationships plus a client success / sales role (self-managed Year 1, $55K Year 2, $75K Year 3) for nonprofit client relationships. If the technical hire leaves, institutional knowledge leaves with them.

Option 2 reduces the upfront fee by $20,000 by removing the documentation and training package. The operational responsibility Orr Group inherits is identical to Option 1. You have direct access to 2516 when something is urgent but you're still responsible for knowing when to call it.

Variable cost risk: At 8 hours/month of support engagement, Option 2 generates ~$16,800/year in additional fees. A bad incident month could mean $5,000–$10,000+ in a single invoice. The 10-hour monthly cap in the contract protects the worst case but variable costs require active management.

Staffing note: Same as Option 1 part-time technical operator required ($65K/yr). Client success / sales role required (self-managed Year 1, hired Year 2+). Attrition risk on technical hire is real and carries cost.

A note on the long-term comparison

Options 1 and 2 show lower net margin in Year 1 to Year 3 on paper when the true cost of technical staffing, attrition, security incidents, and platform maintenance is included. The numbers on the Investment page make this comparison explicit. Option 3 costs more in the first year but it removes operational risk, eliminates technical staff dependency, and lets Orr Group focus entirely on growing the client base rather than managing infrastructure. The platform also stays current and secure permanently, which Options 1 and 2 cannot guarantee.
