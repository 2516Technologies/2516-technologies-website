Requirement 03

Virtual consultant, interactive avatar, chatbot, and native CRM

Four distinct product surfaces: a virtual AI consultant delivering Orr Group's expertise on demand; an interactive avatar for human-feeling engagement; a Claude-powered chatbot for Q&A; and a native CRM for managing nonprofit contacts. Each has different technical requirements and a specific build sequence.

Our initial assessment

⚠ The prototype cannot support 1,000 users

The Claude-based prototype you and Ari built is storing data in flat JSON with no real backend. That works for two people demoing a concept it fails at any meaningful concurrency and cannot support billing, multi-tenancy, or AI training at any scale. This is a rebuild, not an upgrade. That's what prototypes are for.

📱 Mobile isn't optional for this market

Small nonprofit staff are frequently part-time and phone-first. A platform that doesn't work on a 375px screen will lose the majority of its target users before they log in a second time. Mobile-first is a core architecture decision, not a CSS adjustment made at the end.

🔢 Four surfaces need a clear build sequence

Building all four simultaneously is the fastest way to deliver something that looks bad and doesn't work. The right approach is phased stable foundation first, AI features second, after the infrastructure is proven.

✅ The concept is proven the prototype did its job

The Claude API integration works. The UX vision is clear. You have something to demo to stakeholders right now. None of that work is wasted the prompt architecture and CRM interface intent carry forward into the production build.

💡 The platform needs to be built to grow, not just to launch

In 12 months you'll want behavior analytics. In 18 months, custom AI model training. In 24 months, donor scoring. Every one of those capabilities requires clean, structured data collected from day one. The architecture we're proposing makes all of it possible without a rebuild.

You're building something the nonprofit sector genuinely doesn't have. The vision is right. The market is ready. The prototype has proven the concept works. What's missing is the infrastructure to deliver it at scale reliably, on mobile, without breaking.
