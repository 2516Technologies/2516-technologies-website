Option 01
Build, Deliver & Train
Full ownership transfer. 2516 builds and hands over. Orr Group owns and operates independently post-delivery.

How it works

2516 designs, builds, and deploys the full Phase 1 platform. At handover, you receive the complete codebase, deployment documentation, runbooks for common operations, and three structured training sessions — one for administrators, one for end users, and one technical session for whoever manages the platform internally.

After handover, Orr Group owns the platform outright. You manage your own AWS account, your own deployments, and your own ongoing development. 2516 is available for future work on a separate engagement basis, but has no ongoing obligation.

This option is best suited for organizations that have internal technical capability — or plan to hire it — and want complete control over their platform without any ongoing vendor relationship.

How it works

Same build quality as Option 1, but with a slightly lower upfront fee in exchange for a continuing hourly engagement. After handover, 2516 remains available at $175/hour for troubleshooting, feature requests, third-party integrations, and incident response. AWS costs are passed through at cost.

You own the platform. You control the roadmap. But when something breaks or you want a new feature, 2516 is a phone call away — billing only for actual hours worked. No monthly minimum, no retainer.

This option works well when Orr Group wants ownership without wanting to be fully responsible for all technical decisions. It keeps 2516 involved without a long-term commitment on either side.

Option 03
Licensed SaaS + Retainer
2516 builds, owns, and operates. Orr Group licenses access and scales freely without IT burden.
Onboarding + recurring
$20,000

How it works

2516 builds the platform and retains ownership of the IP. Orr Group licenses access on a per-user monthly basis. The retainer covers all AWS infrastructure management, SLA-backed uptime, ongoing maintenance, and platform updates. AWS costs are absorbed by 2516 — Orr Group never receives an AWS bill.

Orr Group gets a fully managed, always-current platform with no internal IT burden. 2516 handles incidents, deploys updates, manages database scaling, and monitors uptime. When something breaks at 2am, 2516 fixes it — not Orr Group's staff.

At 1,000 users, this generates approximately $168,000/year in license revenue plus $36,000/year in retainer revenue — a strong recurring income stream for 2516, and a predictable, all-inclusive monthly cost for Orr Group. The economics work well for both sides.

Data portability is guaranteed in the contract. If Orr Group ever wants to exit the relationship, they receive a full data export in standard formats and 180 days of transition support. No lock-in without recourse.

Why Option 3 is the right call

The total cost of ownership over three years tells the real story. Under Options 1 and 2, Orr Group absorbs AWS infrastructure costs, internal IT staffing, platform maintenance, annual security reviews, and the cost of re-engaging 2516 every time a new feature is needed. Those costs are real, recurring, and easy to underestimate.

Under Option 3, all of that is included in the retainer. On a risk-adjusted 3-year basis, Option 3 is frequently competitive with Option 1 and cheaper than Option 2. The Year 1 investment looks higher on paper — but the actual total cost of operating the platform independently is rarely as low as it appears.

There's also a strategic argument: Orr Group's core competency is nonprofit AI consulting, not platform operations. Every hour CJ's team spends managing AWS, debugging deployments, or chasing 2516 for hourly support is an hour not spent serving clients, developing the product vision, or growing the business. Option 3 keeps Orr Group in its zone of genius.
